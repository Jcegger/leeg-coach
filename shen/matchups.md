## Extreme threats

### Sylas
42.6% WR over 465 games.
He steals your R and uses it better than you — never R an ally while Sylas is alive and his E is up, he will chain-CC your teammate after teleporting in. Trade windows exist post-6 when his E is on cooldown; bait it with a step-in, then E-Q when he's committed. He out-sustains you with stolen heals, so short trades only.

### K'Sante
43.1% WR over 1,779 games.
He out-sustains and out-duels in extended trades. His W makes him unstoppable and absorbs your taunt — don't E him when W is up. Play for short Grasp procs and R value. Do not all-in post-6 without poke; he turns the fight with his own R.

### Akali
44.2% WR over 1,338 games.
Pre-6 you can trade relatively even — Q poke and E-Grasp when she steps in. Post-6 she's untouchable in shroud; your E taunts her out of it but you need to predict the re-entry. W blocks her Q hit but not her E dive. Don't fight her in shroud. Save E for when she comes out of stealth to burst you.

### Camille
44.8% WR over 1,856 games.
Her E phases through your W taunt zone — she can still AA you inside it during the second phase of her hook. Short trades only; her Q2 true damage ignores your armor so don't stack armor vs her in lane. E her before she gets her own E off to prevent the cage. Get Thornmail early.

### Gragas
45.0% WR over 1,101 games.
His E interrupts your R channel — do not ult while Gragas has E up and is in range. Trades are manageable with E-Q combos; his passive regen makes extended fights bad for you. Body Slam (E) has a long cooldown early — that's your poke window. Build Second Wind, this is a poke lane.

## Major threats

### Fiora
Her Riposte (W) parries your E — never taunt blindly into a Fiora with W available. The two windows to E aggressively: her W is on cooldown, or she's close enough she can't react in time. When she dashes for a Vital on you, drag through her with your passive shield blocking the hit and trade empowered Qs back. Thornmail early; she heals through extended trades.

### Gwen
Her W (Hallowed Mist) makes her untargetable from outside — your E goes through her but she becomes AA-immune inside it. %HP magic damage means your bulk counts for less than usual. Take Spirit Visage early. Short trades only; her passive (damage mitigation while stacking scissors) means extended fights favor her heavily.

### Irelia
Her E resets on low-HP minions let her stay in melee indefinitely — she follows every disengage. Check her passive stacks before taunting: at 4+ stacks she wins any trade. Bait her E to a minion, then E-Q when she overextends. She spikes hard at first item; try to establish a lead before she assembles her kit.

### Renekton
Dominant levels 2-3: his W stun into Q combo removes most of your HP before your E connects. Let level 1 pass safely. At level 2, your E is your main escape from his stun burst. W-block his Q. Post-6 he loses kill threat as you scale; play for R value and focus on farm.

## Even

### Darius
Shen favored (~51% WR). Dodge his E-Q hook by standing very close to him or at max range — the sweet spot range pulls you in and heals him. Always block his W spin with your W. Don't taunt forward unless you have a level or HP advantage. Levels 3-5 are your best window before he gets stats.

### Garen
Farm lane. Position your spirit blade above you to force short Q-through trades. Block his Q silence with W, trade empowered Qs, E through him to disengage while his judgment (E) is spinning. Neither kills the other; play for objective control via R.

### Jax
His Counter Strike (E) blocks all your Q-empowered AAs for 2 seconds — don't auto during it. Trade window: immediately after his E ends, E-Q while he's exposed. He spikes hard at first item (Trinity or Shojin); play conservatively around that spike window. Post-6 you disengage his ult easily.

### Malphite
Farm lane. He wins extended trades with his passive shield regen; short Grasp procs favor you. His R is one of the game's best teamfight engages — track it and don't get caught in a poor position. Both play toward teamfights; safe early.

### Nasus
Farm matchup — he stacks Q, you R your team. Short Grasp procs while he farms. He becomes dangerous at ~200 stacks in side lane; avoid 1v1 late. Your value in this matchup is entirely in your global R, not in winning the lane.

### Tryndamere
Can't kill him (undying passive); he can't burst you (your bulk). Farm lane. Save W for his crit AA windows — it blocks auto attacks. Your R is extremely valuable here to help teammates being dove by his split pressure.
