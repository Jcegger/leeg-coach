# Mundo — Playbook

## Pros / Cons

**Pros**
- Incredibly tanky — probably the tankiest champ in the game
- Strongest split push of any tank
- Still pops squishies despite tank itemization
- Unique tenacity (passive) makes him the premier face-tanker
- Kayle-level scaling
- Strong blind pick for top
- Generally beats champs that most juggernauts fail into (Jayce, Kennen, Vayne, Teemo)

**Cons**
- One of the worst laning phases in top
- Useless if behind unless team can carry your corpse to 25+ min
- Awkward in higher-elo comps
- No hard CC on a tank
- Passive IS his tenacity — heavy slows, silences, terrain bypass it
- Loses some matchups other juggernauts crush (e.g., Kled, Riven)

## Ability cheat-sheet

### Passive — Goes Where He Pleases
- Bonus HP regen scales heavily with level
- Periodically tanks the next CC (4% current HP cost, drops a canister); walking over canister restores 4% max HP and reduces CD by 15s
- CD: 60s at lvl 1 → 15s at lvl 15 (canister pickup *fully resets* CD past 15)
- Both halves of the passive are very weak early — extreme per-level scaling

### Q — Infected Bonesaw
- % current HP magic damage + 40% slow for 2s
- Costs flat HP to cast (not mana)
- Hits champion or large monster: heals 100% of the cast cost (free)
- Hits anything else (minion): heals only 50% of cast cost (net loss)
- **A missed Q at rank 1 costs ~50 HP. A third of a pot.**

### W — Heart Zapper
- AoE magic DPS over 3s, stores damage taken as grey health
- **First 0.25s stores almost all damage. After that, only 25%.**
- Recast deals burst + heals 50% of grey HP
- Recast hitting champion or large monster: heals 100% of grey HP (always look for this)

### E — Blunt Force Trauma
- Empowered next auto, more damage at lower HP
- AA reset
- If kills the target: flings a medical bag dealing damage
- **Passive: bonus AD based on max HP** — Mundo can have more AD than the ADC late game
- One-shots casters in waves once you have Warmog's

### R — Maximum Dosage
- 120s CD at every rank (this is a problem)
- Rank 1 is weak; rank 3 is one of the strongest ults in the game
- Bonus health based on missing HP — ult low for max value
- **Don't ult for sustain in lane** post-rework (CD too long, heal too small now)

## Lane phase

- One of the worst in top. Plan to give up some farm to stay in XP range.
- **Almost always shove.** You can't be frozen on — without minion cover, the enemy eats free cleavers.
- Cleaver discipline: hitting champ = free, hitting minion = 50% net loss. A missed Q is -50 HP. Don't spam.
- If enemy backs and tower is open: auto + E for 2 platings (after 5 min).
- Big wave crashing into your tower at 6+ = dive risk. Watch for jungler.
- Activate W *right before* enemy main damage move, not at start of fight.
- Reactivate W on a champion at almost any cost (double heal).
- **Do NOT ult to heal in lane.**

## Mid game

**Definition:** Heartsteel + full boots OR level 11, whichever comes first.

- **Heartsteel spike:** enough HP regen to spam cleavers. Trade pattern = poke at range with Q, run in to proc Heartsteel with E, repeat.
- **Level 11 spike:** R becomes ~2x stronger. Play aggressively unless very behind.
- You can 2v1 enemy top + jungler in most matchups (assuming neither hard-counters your kit).
- Split push to gather resources: tower gold, wave XP, camps. Mundo's wave clear is disgusting, tower damage is highest among tanks, and E does heavy damage to monsters.
- Goal: accelerate to late game ASAP.

**Optimal R combo:** activate W → wait for grey HP to stack → R while low → reactivate W to heal off grey. You're suddenly half HP with maximum bonus HP.

## Late game (lvl 16, 3+ items)

- Rank 3 R + Spirit Visage = god mode. You can stat-check entire teams alone if you're on level/CS (9–10 CS/min, level 16 while team is 13–14).
- Past level 15, **always pick up your canister** — full CD reset means you can build zero tenacity.
- If behind: still tanky enough to force focus. Run at the ADC. Two outcomes:
  1. They focus you → team kills theirs (4–5s of free damage even vs tank shredders)
  2. They ignore you → you actually kill the ADC

**Splitting vs grouping when behind:**
- Mundo is an Illaoi/Sion-type splitter (kill whoever shows up), not a Garen/Trynd (run away).
- If you can't 1v1 the responder, **don't split. Group.**
- "If you don't know what to do, group. It isn't always the best option but it's very rarely a bad choice."
- Splitting is often a flat-out bad choice for Mundo.

## Hyper late (everyone level 18, full build)

- Scaling slows unless you manage Heartsteel stacks well.
- ADC will start to shred you — can't completely frontline anymore.
- Either play with team backing you up, or commit to split push but more carefully.
- **The flaw:** without R, you can't teamfight. R stays at 120s CD at every rank, and Mundo barely builds AH.
- **Make ult worth it.** If you're going to die anyway, *don't* copium-ult — save it so post-respawn you have impact. Exception: stalling for Baron/objective is worth ulting away.

## Teamfighting

### Position
- Front-to-back. Stand *between* your carries and enemy threats.
- You're not an engage tank — you're a face-tanker. Distribute damage taken so team outputs.
- Stay close enough to cleaver-poke + threaten engage; far enough that the enemy can't collapse on you.

### Engage trigger
- Team in position AND R is up. **No R = do not frontline a real fight.**
- Run at the ADC, not the tank. Tanking the enemy frontline does nothing.

### Cleaver poke before engage
- Q from max range to slow priority targets (Approach Velocity + Q slow + Swifties + Dead Man's momentum = absurd MS to stick).
- Landed cleaver on enemy carry = your engage signal.

### W in fights
- Don't pre-cast walking in. Wait for engage/burst attempt, *then* W.
- Always look for the champion-recast for double heal.

### R timing
- Combo: low HP → W (store grey) → R (huge bonus HP) → reactivate W (massive heal) → keep punching.

### Vision
- Zero disengage on standard build (no Phase Rush). Ward flanks before grouping at objectives. If caught alone walking up, you die.

### Objectives
- Drake/Baron: be the body that soaks smite-contest damage. Job = "make obj uncontestable without enemy dying first."
- Cleavers do % current HP — great chunk on objectives and frontline tanks.

## Build-specific notes (Warmog's path)

- **No Spirit Visage** in this build → W heal is weaker than standard. Compensate by reactivating W more aggressively on champs, don't get greedy.
- **No Unending Despair** → less passive AoE damage. Damage comes from cleaver poke + Heartsteel proc + Titanic auto-resets — actually swing autos in fights, don't just exist.
- **Dead Man's momentum** stacks while moving + applies on-hit slow → engage from the longest path possible (not a straight line) to stack before arrival.
- **Titanic + Heartsteel** wants you in melee. Hit the closest squishy.
- **Cleaver to last-hit camps on the way to obj** so you're not running on empty HP into a fight.

## The discipline rule

> Mundo doesn't have the same options as if you were doing well when you hit 16. Running into the enemy team alone to kill the ADC will just result in you running around for 10 seconds until your ult runs out and then you die.
>
> But teamfights are still really solid — forcing the enemy to kill you first takes 4–5 seconds even vs full tank-shredders (except Kog'Maw, who actually obliterates you).
