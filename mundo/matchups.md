## Extreme threats

### Ambessa

ALWAYS Q MAX Treat her like a harder Renekton, trades are short and punishing. After level 6: she can all-in off any small mistake, so respect her ult cooldown at all times (its pretty long early). Runes: Grasp → best if you know the matchup and can reliably dodge her Q. You can go even or slightly ahead in trades this way. Phase Rush → safer if you’re unsure; makes it much harder to get run down and feed. Her e and ult are massive slows and she usually builds voltaic into mundo so phase rush is the only way to make it playable if you cant straight up win a fight after laning phase Dodge her Q2 whenever possible, if you can't dodge at all just stand next to minions it does 50% damage to anything past the first target Look to outrange her poke and avoid any trade unless you have w up (but keep in mind if you use w to trade well, if she ults you after while ur w is on cooldown ur just dead) Like I said, she doesn’t have hard CC, but her slows are extremely heavy and can trap you if you misstep. So you cant just run past her later on like you can most champs in teamfights Matchup status: high-risk lane. Manageable if you dodge Q and respect her ult, but one slip post-6 can end the lane on the spot and she is one of the most snowbally top laners in the game

### Bel Veth

You are the exact champion archetype she thrives on, a tanky brawler with no mobility or CC. She isn't dangerous if she isn’t fed outside of a completely isolated 1v1, BUT: due to the nature of the champion bel'veth will be fed in like half the games she's in, atleast in high elo. And if you're top that's obviously completely out of your control. Build Bramble to prevent her from using you as a heal sponge/making the 1v1 somewhat playable if she isnt fed. Matchup status: extremely punishing when she’s ahead. Pretty much yi on steroids and is way less dependant on item strength

### Gnar

Build: no-warmogs
PREFERENCE, I USUALLY E MAX Not much to say, just keep in mind even late game if you step up too far he can chase and kill you indefinitely. Mega gnar is actually not that much of an issue but mini gnar is basically harder vayne (his w passive gives him a constant ms steroid and magic max health damage). Post 6 if you land a cleaver in lane against him and he doesn't have rage you can look to all-in (you have to juke the boomerang) but beyond that it's just a scale lane where you pray to god someone on your team gets fed

### Gwen

ALWAYS Q MAX WITH PHASE RUSH, BUT IF FOR WHATEVER REASON YOU WENT GRASP YOU CAN E MAX Standard approaches to gwen (i.e., grasp and brute forcing stuff) are very punishing, but careful poke and attrition trades make the lane manageable. This is the main matchup that separates Mundo OTP's and Mundo learners/filled ADCs. Use Phase Rush + Scorch for heavy Q poke. If she’s not a Gwen main counterpicking, you can land most cleavers on her predictive E’s. Your goal is to get out of lane even, so early survival is key. You don't have to win. Once lane is even, Warmog’s makes sidelanes extremely easy. Combo → Phase Rush away → heal up. She’ll often be around half health while you’re full. ATP you can all-in her AVOID FULL DAMAGE BUILDS!!!!, I know intuitively it would seem like the best build into her but you won’t reliably 1v1 her outside of attrition trades anyway. Warmog’s alone is sufficient for that. phase rush is NEEDED. The slow resistance is what ruins her kill pressure in lane. Otherwise she just kills you off cd with ult unless you stand under tower, and you cant split properly lastly, this was all for Gwen top. Gwen jungle is unplayable piss cancer. Pray your jungler doesnt lose, and if you're jungle... shoulda banned it. Matchup status: easiest “level 5” threat (Before you come at me saying fiora is easier, I don't even consider her a lvl 5 threat unless its a challenger fiora otp). In top atleast in terms of not solo losing the game, completely unplayable in jungle. Still punishing if misplayed, but proper poke and attrition trading keep lane and sidelanes manageable but if you have the worse team you have ZERO way of carrying. Gwen is my perma-ban and I suggest you do the same.

### Illaoi

Always Q max. Playstyle depends on comp, If your team has range/poke, you can turtle and let them handle her later as long as you dont giga feed. If your team is melee-heavy, she doesn’t really get outscaled so you need to come out of lane even. Trading: Hypothetically, if you dodge every E and land every cleaver, you win, but this is very hard in practice. You can’t rely on blocking E with minions, since you still lose an all-in even if she misses so she'll just spam w on you. If you read general guides or most top guides, it'll usually say to fight in your wave to deny her e, mundo doesn't have this luxury so throw that notion away. THIS is the real reason the matchup is so bad. Stay behind the wave and look for safe Q trades. Break tentacles whenever you get the chance. Early game: Landing her E pre-6 doesn’t matter much if she has no ult to follow up. You can actually kill her pre-6 if you play it well and poke consistently. Keep in mind post-6 if she slow pushes multiple waves she can really easily 1f1 you under tower so you have to thin the wave Matchup status: very punishing past 6, but surprisingly killable early. Respect her ult zone and manage trades around wave positioning to keep the lane playable.

### Kog Maw

not a top laner but he's by far the worst adc in terms of raw combat for you. I like to ban him when im last pick (no one blinds gwen) and my team has no assassins. Intuitively you'd think it'd be vayne but her low range and worse synergies with enchanters + your passive ruining her condemn which is like half her power budget makes it really not that bad of an adc assuming you run her down quickly. Kogmaw on the other hand just stands there and kills you in 3 seconds even at lvl 16 while you dont do nearly enough damage to him if he has an enchanter

### Tryndamere

Skill max: preference, but generally skewed toward E max. If he plays disrespectfully (random all-ins): Poke with cleavers, farm safely. Very easy to punish; you scale much harder in side lane and teamfights. If the enemy adc is a crit user make sure to go randuins 3rd item If he plays well (decent proxy/split macro + Ravenous build): Try to thin waves when you can, but expect to get poked out if you overdo it. Similarly to Aatrox you have to feign confidence by landing cleavers whenever he steps past the minion wave. Conditioning the enemy tryndamere to think he gets punished for being aggressive makes the lane WAY easier. Trading/tips: Keep W active as long as possible Avoid extended fights early; tryndamere has the best laning phase out of any top laner, or arguably any champion in the game. In return he falls off a complete cliff versus tanks after like 2 items if you aren't as confident you can rush a warden's mail, it neutralizes the lane, but you can't really skirmish if you do this Runes: Grasp → if confident you can turtle and play safe. Phase Rush → if you expect to get bullied or frozen on. Matchup status: very manageable in low elo and scales heavily in your favor. Strong Trynd macro can be tough, although sort of like teemo or heimerdinger if your jungler shows any interest in top he'll calm down and let you play the game

## Major threats

### Aatrox

ALWAYS Q MAX Always dodge Q, if he lands it, your passive procs, which against his version of CC is completely useless Trade back with Q when he E’s forward. The matchup is substantially more playable if you condition the enemy aatrox to think he has to take a cleaver if he wants to q you When his passive is up, Don’t walk up, just farm with cleaver, wait for him to use it on a minion. If his passive hits you, he heals ~25% HP and deals abunch of damage, its based on max health Don’t rush bramble, it won’t win the 1v1 and delays your spikes, the main one being warmogs + giant belt (generally not a massive believer in this tech but against aatrox its good). Instead, build Bramble after Heartsteel + Warmog’s, or immediately if skipping Warmog’s (not recommended vs Aatrox). Matchup status: still your hardest lane. Prime warmog’s rush used to help, but now it’s back to being the worst matchup. I just ban it. Win condition: poke, dodge, if he's below 1/4 of his HP and misses a q you can all-in him pre 6 ill keep this here because its good for learning the lane and it might be true down the road BUT. Currently the matchup got substantially easier if you know what you're doing: ignite becoming viable on mundo + more forgiving back timers made the matchup a lot more playable. You outscale pretty quickly now so you just have to play early well. Nowadays every other time i go against aatrox i first blood him lol skip bramble until after heartsteel + warmogs, ignite is your source of antiheal for all-ins poke poke poke back if you're in kill threshold (its pretty low you have to be like 5% hp), if you ever die your game is completely over its that simple! Just dont eat every q early and you're on track to outscale. The important aspect is to NOT PLAY TO JUST NOT LOSE LANE. Afk farming with cleaver makes this lane IMPOSSIBLE. You have to play confidently and threaten all-ins.

### Fiora

Always Q max. This is probably going to be the longest matchup description, because depending on how you play it, it's either one of mundo's worst matchups or a matchup you pray autofills lock into you. On-paper difficulty: Extremely punishing, same as Gwen. Lane can feel terrible if misplayed. However, landing most of your cleavers lets you poke her down and survive lane. There are 3 distinct rune pages and playstyles you can try into Fiora, I don't think one is better than the other. I personally usually go grasp. Standard Grasp Page: Trading: Wait until she tries to Q in for a vital, then Cleaver → Auto → E → Walk away. Walk juuust into her q range and then walk out repeatedly. If you bait a q out you can look for a trade, her q CD when it doesnt hit anything is pretty long If she’s low and you’re healthy, play aggressively: shove waves, poke under turret, and bait her Parry for tower dives. If you get a lead, you can 1v1 her until she hits 2–3 items. After that, trades become a ring-around-the-rosie minigame where you slow her with cleavers and keep her away from vitals speaking of which, if she ults you hug a wall so she cant proc the heal. The heal is disgusting. RUSH. BRAMBLE. VEST. I don't even do it into aatrox, I understand the power of mundo item spikes, in 99% of games you should be rushing your first legendary item, but into Fiora or Irelia you just autolose without bramble. If you want more consistency for less carry potential you can go bramble + another cloth armor. Sidelane & teamfights: Fiora cannot tower dive you post-laning phase, so her splits aren’t uncontested. Like most split pushers you need feign confidence so that she gets the idea that if she goes for tower while you're there, she will die. Fiora, while strong, is really predictable so if you know mundo's limits you can play like a rabid dog til your low and then heal with warmogs, it scares the **** out of fiora's that arent super confident You outscale her 10-1 in teamfights Risk: One mistake and Fiora punishes hard. Grasp is the main rune page that lets you WIN the lane, but if you make one real mistake or godforbid you die into a fiora, the game is over. Hypothetically if you play perfectly though grasp gives the fiora the least agency over the lane which is why I like it TLDR Grasp is ideal in low elo where Fiora players also take Grasp or are just generally not very good Bramble ensures even hyper-late-game 1v1s go in your favor with Heartsteel up, and you win all-ins assuming both ult and you know how to deny vitals. Gaining lane control is key, Fiora has better sidelane tempo, so respecting her rotations or making her match you/setting up waves so that she has to go to objectives is vital. Heh. First Strike Page: Lane play: Farm with cleavers and establish boundaries by landing them whenever she walks past the wave sorta like aatrox or trynd. Rare matchup where Titanic can outperform Warmog’s but keep in mind like my warmogs section says you do NOT build both. Strategy: Safer if you’re unsure of Mundo’s limits or are scared of Fiora. Harder to feed lane, but trades generally end in her favor unless you COMPLETELY outplay her (i.e., dodge her q) TLDR: ***** rune if u dont want to feed Phase rush: kind of a mix of first strike and grasp. You play lane similarly to first strike with the caviet that you can play sidelanes way more aggressively later on without having to win lane. Unless you know how to abuse phase rush late though I would just go first strike, it scales a lot better if you're only gonna teamfight Summary: Grasp → risky, stronger if you know the matchup and want to punish mistakes/need to carry. First Strike → more consistent but slightly in her favor; better against skilled Fiora OTPs with aggressive PTA-style setups. Phase rush → you're a split push freakazoid

### Kled

Always Q max. Pre-midscope lethality Kled was one of Mundo’s worst lanes, it was completely unplayable and was previously the worst matchup on this guide with the caveat that kled players are terrible Current bruiser Kled (health stacking, lower damage) is much more manageable for mundo. Luckily, kled players being terrible hasn't changed. His Q no longer applies Grievous Wounds, so he must build Executioner’s now That all being said the lane is still Kled-favored. Turtle in lane, farm safely. Use Q to keep him dismounted whenever possible to reduce his threat. Similarly to Illaoi you might see people say to keep him juuust in dismount range to threaten an all-in, but Mundo loses regardless so it's better to just keep him dismounted. Kled players will generally focus on remounting so thats free time to farm Matchup status: Kled still has the edge, but the rework shifted this from unwinnable to playable. With patience and good Qs, you can stabilize and scale.

### Master Yi

He's the third worst jungler (behind Gwen and Bel’Veth). Almost impossible to 1v1 unless you are massively ahead and he is far behind, or if he skips BOTRK. Cleaver doesn’t slow him during ult, so you have to be more mindful of splitting since if he shows up you can't run away. You NEED a bramble. If you're jungle I would honestly rush it Teamfights: Yi’s hardest counter is CC, which you don't provide. You’re mostly at his mercy, a competent Yi can easily dismantle your team if positioned well while you watch from the cuck chair. So to play teamfights you have to force him to focus you by braindead running into him or his team. If you do it properly while ur team is ready, 1 of 2 things will happen: 1. Yi focuses on you at which point hopefully someone on your team blows him up 2. (more likely) he runs past you towards your team, so you're 1v4ing his team and he's 1v4ing your team. This is generally favored towards you unless he's fed and can actually 1v4, since you can stall a lot longer than he can. Meta considerations: Yi isn’t always a level 5 threat; his power heavily depends on meta shifts, items (Bork/Kraken), or exploitable runes. He is extremely item-reliant, so his strength can swing drastically based on build and rune efficiency more than someone like j4. When yi is weak you just build a bramble and smack him down Matchup status: punishing and largely uncontrollable. You can only mitigate risk by ensuring he falls behind early with good standard jg macro or capitalizing on mistakes, otherwise, he dictates the pace of the game and forces you to make dumb plays to have a chance.

### Sett

Always Q max. Play very slowly/carefully. Sett’s bully range is high due to his MS steroid, think Garen with a 350 Q range. Hard lane, but manageable by farming safely with cleavers. If you don't die in lane you outscale him pretty hard outside of a sidelane Teamfighting: Even with your passive, his ult won’t bring you along, but your bonus HP conversion applies to anyone he ults into. Proper positioning is really important, if he ults your HP into your backline, the teamfight can be lost. This applies to any high hp tank (think those tyler1 cho clips) but its more-so important as mundo since your team is more oblivious to this so they might stand near his ult impact Runes: Phase Rush + Inspiration → safe if confident or in low elo where Sett misplays zoning. Phase rush + resolve → safer than inspiration with less pay-off late First Strike + Resolve → safest if you just want to turtle in lane and dont want to bother with phase rush mechanics Avoid Grasp, to proc it you have to go into melee range which is never a good idea Matchup status: standard bully lane. Patience, proper cleaver usage, and careful positioning in teamfights make this matchup manageable although he snowballs out of control if you mess up

### Udyr

Q max if he lanes normally. E max if he perma-proxies. Udyr as a proxy threat: Main proxy champion that can heavily punish Mundo along with tryndamere. Udyr + jungler can tower dive you more easily than champions like Singed. Threat level depends heavily on Elo and jungle coordination. Low Elo: Minor threat, outrange, farm, and outscale. Only take cleaver trades if completely free, udyr sustain and shielding is really high. Focus on safe farming and scaling. ### Renekton

ALWAYS Q MAX If he dashes onto you, Cleaver → Auto → E → walk away. When he activates W, if he's in auto range just use your own w and walk into him immediately, you'll nullify his damage since renekton uses his entire damage combo within the stun. His threat range is high due to double dash, so be extra cautious when low. super important, reiterating: reactivating W gives effectively ~90% damage reduction since Renekton uses his full kit in a short window. This is the key reason the matchup is easy. You outscale him heavily in almost all aspects except peel. Runes: Grasp is recommended, trade when he dashes in and leverage your superior late-game scaling. You can't bully him or anything but he has to bully you to stop you from giga-outscaling him, and he loses trades if he dashes into you, so he's put in a really bad spot if you confidently turtle Phase Rush is less necessary; by late game, you should be able to 2v1 him and his jungle if needed. Matchup status: lane is difficult if you don't respect him or play TOO scared, but really easy with proper trades and W timing. Mid/late game the matchup is a joke
