# Sivir — Build

Source: [heeiseenbeerg's "The Ultimate Sivir Build" (16.08, April 2026)](https://www.mobafire.com/league-of-legends/build/16-08-the-ultimate-sivir-build-648305). Notes are the author's; quoted/paraphrased from their tooltips.

## Summoner spells

**Barrier + Flash.** "These should almost always be your summoner spells."

## Runes

**Primary — Precision:** Lethal Tempo · Presence of Mind · Legend: Bloodline · Cut Down

**Secondary — Sorcery:** Manaflow Band · Gathering Storm

**Shards:** +10% Attack Speed · +9 Adaptive (5.4 AD) · +65 Base Health

### Why Manaflow Band over Absolute Focus

> Most people go Absolute Focus but I like Manaflow Band because it gives enough mana so you never have to worry about it (especially without Essence Reaver). Absolute Focus only works above 70% health and the extra AD it gives isn't very significant. Only Q procs your Manaflow Band stacks — try to avoid using it without hitting the enemy when you're still stacking it, and wait for it to reset before using Q again.

### Sorcery vs Inspiration secondary

> Inspiration secondary is viable this season thanks to cashback, but it was nerfed earlier this season. The reason I recommend Sorcery over Inspiration: you won't ever have to worry about mana after stacking Manaflow Band, which can be a concern when building Yun Tal over Essence Reaver. Still, if you feel more comfortable with Inspiration second then go for it.

## Starting items

**Standard:** Doran's Blade + Health Potion
> Doran's Blade is the standard starting item. It gives good early pressure potential and some sustain (the vamp also works on your Q and W ricochets, unlike life steal).

**Alternative (losing matchups):** Cull + Health Potion
> A good start into losing matchups where you're looking to not fall far behind, helped by the gold refund from Cull.

## First back

**Ideal:** B. F. Sword
> Always buy B.F. Sword on first back when you can. Obviously you can buy more if possible including Boots/Refillable.

**If you can't afford BF Sword:** Long Sword first, then any of: Boots, Scout's Slingshot, Dagger, Refillable Potion, Control Ward.
> Get as much as you can towards Yun Tal and/or Boots/Refillable. Always buy a Long Sword before anything else.

## Core build

1. **Yun Tal Wildarrows** (1st item)
2. **Boots** (tier 2 before 2nd item, definitely before 3rd)
3. **Infinity Edge** *or* **Navori Flickerblade** (2nd item — depends on boots)
4. **Lord Dominik's Regards** *or* **Mortal Reminder** (3rd or 4th)
5. **Stormrazor** (3rd or 4th, completes 100% crit)
6. **Pick two** of: Guardian Angel, Bloodthirster, Mercurial Scimitar, Maw of Malmortius

### Yun Tal first

> I find Yun Tal is a more well-rounded item than Essence Reaver, which is why I always build it first. We don't have to worry about mana thanks to Manaflow Band.

### Boots

> Prioritize building components towards first item before upgrading to tier 2 boots. Aim to have tier 2 boots completed before 2nd item, and definitely before 3rd item.
>
> Berserker's — best into mixed damage (most of the time).
> Plated Steelcaps — predominantly AD damage.
> Mercury's Treads — predominantly AP and/or a lot of CC.

### 2nd item — IE vs Navori

> Build IE second if you went/will go with Berserker's as your boots.
> If you went/will go with Steelcaps/Mercury's, go with Navori second.

### 3rd / 4th — finishing 100% crit

> If you went Navori second, always build IE third into LDR/Mortal Reminder fourth.
>
> If you went IE second, go LDR/Mortal Reminder third and Stormrazor fourth — unless the enemy team builds no armor / has 5 squishies. In that case, build Stormrazor third and LDR/Mortal Reminder fourth.

### LDR vs Mortal Reminder

> Always build LDR if your team has (or will have) 2+ members with grievous wounds (anti-healing) or if the enemy team has close to no healing/life steal. Otherwise build Mortal Reminder. You should be building LDR in about 70% of your games.

### 5th / 6th item

> GA as 5th is ideal in about 90% of games. After GA you'll typically build BT, but you can also choose Scimitar or Maw if you want extra MR or QSS.
>
> Up against heavy AP — settle for Scimitar or Maw 5th. Scimitar is usually better unless the enemy has low CC (also: QSS doesn't work on airborne) or no scary hard-CC ability.
>
> All-AP team — build both Scimitar and Maw as last items.

### Don't forget Elixir of Wrath

> Buy when you're at 5 – 5.5 items and just before a decisive teamfight. The elixir gives incredible value for the cost while it's active.

## Example full builds

### Standard
Yun Tal · Berserker's · Infinity Edge · Lord Dominik's · Stormrazor · Guardian Angel · Bloodthirster · Elixir of Wrath
> Very well-rounded with lots of damage and sustain thanks to GA + BT. LDR and Mortal Reminder are interchangeable.

### vs Heavy AP
Yun Tal · Mercury's Treads · Navori Flickerblade · Infinity Edge · Lord Dominik's · Mercurial Scimitar · Guardian Angel · Elixir of Wrath
> Great tankiness against AP burst with good damage. Swap Scimitar for Maw if the enemy team doesn't have much CC. Vs all-AP, build both Scimitar and Maw as last items instead of GA.

### vs Heavy AD
Yun Tal · Plated Steelcaps · Navori Flickerblade · Infinity Edge · Lord Dominik's · Guardian Angel · Bloodthirster · Elixir of Wrath
> Great tankiness against heavy AD with good damage.

## Situational item swaps (quick reference)

- Plated Steelcaps over Berserker's → Navori over IE — heavy AD
- Mercury's Treads over Berserker's → Navori over IE — heavy AP / CC
- Mortal Reminder over Lord Dominik's — enemy has significant healing
- Mercurial Scimitar over Guardian Angel — heavy AP or hard CC (provides QSS)
- Maw of Malmortius over Guardian Angel — AP team with low CC
- Both Scimitar + Maw as last 2 — all-AP team
